What concepts of "fairness" (from class) are (approximately) satisfied by this classifier? Explain.

  1. Equality in Accuracy: accuracy is not the same, but very close (0.68 versus 0.64)
  2. Statistical Parity: the average predicted outcome for whites is 0.08 and for non-whites it is 0.16, so twice the one for white $\implies$ statistical parity is not satisfied
  3. Error Rate Balance: for whites fp rate is 0.05 and fn rate is 0.86; for non-whites fp rate is 0.11 and fn rate is 0.75, therefore the algorithm does not achieve error rate balance
  4. Predictive Parity: precision for whites is 0.57 and for non-whites it is 0.60; the negative predictive value for whites is 0.69 and for non-whites it is 0.65. Therefore, predictive parity is almost achieved as the values for the two groups are close but not the same
  5. Treatment equality: the fp/fn ratio for white is 0.12, that is almost twice that of non-whites, equal to 0.23. Therefore, treatment equality is not achieved.
